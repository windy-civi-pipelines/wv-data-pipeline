Red = new code to be enacted SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION 19(By Senators Phillips, Stuart, and Jeffries)[Introduced March 14, 2025] Requesting the Division of Highways name bridge number 23-016/83-000.02 (23A157) (37.75836, -81.84628) locally known as BULL HOLLOW BEAM SPAN, crossing BUFFALO CREEK, the "U.S. Army ST4 Gregory K. Stephens Memorial Bridge".Whereas, Gregory Kent Stephens lived a life worthy of remembrance; andWhereas, Gregory K. Stephens was born October 28, 1954, to Carmenita Stephens in Huntington, Cabell County, West Virginia; andWhereas, Gregory K. Stephens enlisted in the U.S. Army on September 23, 1974, and completed Basic Combat Training at Fort Bragg, North Carolina; andWhereas, U.S. Army ST4 Stephens worked as a Food Service Specialist, attained the rank of Specialist 4th class, and received an award for noteworthy performance of duty while assigned as Field Team Chief of B Battery, 2nd Battalion, 59th Air Defense Artillery; andWhereas, U.S. Army ST4 Stephens’ competence and dedication contributed to the successful accomplishments of the mission of "Old Ironsides" in the Federal Republic of Germany; andWhereas, U.S. Army ST4 Stephens was awarded the National Defense Service Medal, Army Achievement Medal, Good Conduct Medal (3D AWD), the Army Service Ribbon, Overseas Service Ribbon, NCO Professional Development Ribbon "1", and qualified as (EXPERT) on the M-16 rifle during his service; andWhereas, U.S. Army ST4 Stephens was honorably discharged on August 18th, 1983; andWhereas, Following discharge, U.S. Army ST4 Stephens stayed in the Fort Polk, Louisiana, area where he completed truck driving school and began a 20-year career in the field; andWhereas, U.S. Army ST4 Stephens relocated to Low Ash in Logan County, West Virginia, where he married his wife, Tammy Joann Wellman, and lived with their blended family for 14 of the 20 years he spent in the area; and Whereas, U.S. Army ST4 Stephens passed away on August 22, 2024, after spending many wonderful years with his family in Logan County, West Virginia; therefore, be it Resolved by the Legislature of West Virginia:That the Division of Highways name bridge number 23-016/83-000.02 (23A157) (37.75836, -81.84628) locally known as BULL HOLLOW BEAM SPAN, crossing BUFFALO CREEK, the "U.S. Army ST4 Gregory K. Stephens Memorial Bridge." and, be it Further Resolved, That the Division of Highways is hereby requested to have made and be placed signs identifying the bridge as the "U.S. Army ST4 Gregory K. Stephens Memorial Bridge"; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the Clerk of the Senate is hereby directed to forward a copy of this resolution to the Commissioner of the Division of Highways.
