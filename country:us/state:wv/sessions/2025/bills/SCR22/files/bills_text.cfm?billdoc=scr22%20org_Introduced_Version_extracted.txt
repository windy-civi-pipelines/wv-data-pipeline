Red = new code to be enacted SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION 22(By Senator Rucker)[Originating in the Committee on Government Organization; reported March 25, 2025] Requesting the Joint Committee on Government and Finance study incentives and policies to promote location of certain beverage container recycling and redemption centers in the state.Whereas, The lack of a glass recycling plant located in West Virginia impacts the state and its citizen in several ways; andWhereas, Transporting glass across state lines from West Virginia to other states adds significant transportation costs, as glass is heavy and fragile, and the cost of moving it long distances is high. This burden is often passed on to consumers or municipalities; andWhereas, Recycling glass locally helps to reduce the carbon footprint associated with transporting the material. If glass must be shipped out of state, it increases emissions from the vehicles used in the process, negating some of the environmental benefits of recycling; andWhereas, Because glass is one of the few materials that can be recycled infinitely without losing quality, it is important to have an efficient recycling system; andWhereas, Local recycling centers create local jobs and economic activity. If the center is located in another state, those economic benefits, such as job creation and the boosting of the local economy, are missed by the community that produces the glass; andWhereas, Offering incentives for local recycling programs, such as bottle bills, deposits, redemptions, or grants to encourage citizens to recycle and entities to locate recycling and redemption facilities in West Virginia would generate economic growth and increased participation by consumers; andWhereas, A thorough examination of current resources, practices, and policies regarding beverage container recycling and redemption centers is necessary to provide informed recommendations for policymakers; therefore, be itResolved by the Legislature of West Virginia:That the Joint Committee on Government and Finance is hereby requested to study incentives and policies to promote location of certain beverage container recycling and redemption centers in the state; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the study should focus on the following elements:1. They types of recyclable materials to include in an incentive program;2. The criteria for redemption centers to collect the materials and dispense the refund;3. Funding or economic incentives to attract recycling facilities to locate in the state; 4. Rules and procedures necessary to govern the recycling and redemption process;5. Target rates for recycling participation; and6. Recommendations for legislative measures, funding opportunities, and partnerships to encourage location of recycling and redemption centers in West Virginia; and, be it  Further Resolved, That the Joint Committee on Government and Finance report to the regular session of the Legislature, 2026, on its findings, conclusions, and recommendations, together with drafts of any legislation necessary to effectuate its recommendations; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the expenses necessary to conduct this study, to prepare a report, and to draft legislation be paid from legislative appropriations to the Joint Committee on Government and Finance.
