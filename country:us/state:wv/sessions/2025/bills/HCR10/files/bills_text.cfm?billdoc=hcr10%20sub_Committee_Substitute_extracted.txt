Red = new code to be enacted Committee SubstituteforHOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION 10(By Delegates Hillenbrand and G. Howell)[Originating in the Committee on Energy and Public Works; Reported on March 10, 2025] Requesting the Division of Highways name a portion of Knobley Road, specifically beginning where U.S. Route 50 meets W.V. Route 9 (39.34980, -78.99220) and ending where W.V. Route 9 meets W.V. Route 18 (39.36571, -78.98110), in Mineral County, the "U.S. Marine Corps PFC Robert Thomas Taylor Memorial Road".Whereas, PFC Robert Thomas Taylor lived a life worthy of remembrance; andWhereas, Robert was born May 2nd, 1950, in Keyser, WV and grew up in Burlington, WV where he attended Burlington Elementary School and graduated from Keyser High School in June of 1968;Whereas, In September 1968, Robert enlisted in the Marine Corps and was sent to Parris Island, South Carolina for boot camp; andWhereas, After boot camp, Robert was sent to Camp Pendleton in California for additional training; andWhereas, After the completion of his training, PFC Taylor would be sent to South Vietnam to serve with L Company, 3rd Battalion, 1st Marine Regiment, 1st Marine Division; andWhereas, PFC Taylor was tragically killed in action on May 12th, 1969, in Quang Nam, South Vietnam while operating an M60 machine gun and serving beside Jeff Latham, who recounted PFC Taylor’s heroics to his family; andWhereas, PFC Robert Thomas Taylor received a Purple Heart, Combat Action Ribbon, Navy and Marine Corps Presidential Unit Citation, National Defense Service Medal, Vietnam Service Medal, Vietnam Campaign Medal, Republic of Vietnam Gallantry Cross with Palm Ribbon, and Republic of Vietnam Civil Actions Honor Medal; andWhereas, It is fitting that an enduring memorial be established to commemorate USMC PFC Robert Thomas Taylor and his contributions to our state and country; therefore, be it Resolved by the Legislature of West Virginia:That the Division of Highways is hereby requested to name a portion of Knobley Road, specifically beginning where U.S. Route 50 meets W.V. Route 9 (39.34980, -78.99220) and ending where W.V. Route 9 meets W.V. Route 18 (39.36571, -78.98110), in Mineral County, the "U.S. Marine Corps PFC Robert Thomas Taylor Memorial Road"; and, be it Further Resolved, That the Division of Highways is hereby requested to have made and be placed signs at both ends identifying the portion of road as the "U.S. Marine Corps PFC Robert Thomas Taylor Memorial Road"; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the Clerk of the House forward a copy of this resolution to the Commissioner of the Division of Highways.
