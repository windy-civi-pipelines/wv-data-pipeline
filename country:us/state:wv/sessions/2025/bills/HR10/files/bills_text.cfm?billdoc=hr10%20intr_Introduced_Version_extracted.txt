Red = new code to be enacted HOUSE RESOLUTION 10 (By Delegates Lewis, Williams, Hornbuckle, Garcia, Hamilton, Young, and Fluharty) [March 19, 2025; Referred to the committee on Health and Human Resourcesthen Rules ] Urging the United States Congress to Extend Enhanced Premium Tax Credits for Health Insurance Premiums Under the Affordable Care Act.Whereas, Affordable health care is a fundamental need for all West Virginia residents, and access to medical care is essential for the well-being, economic stability, and dignity of individuals and families; andWhereas, The Federal Affordable Care Act has significantly reduced the number of uninsured state residents, enabling tens of thousands of families, self-employed individuals, hourly workers, and small business owners to secure health insurance and access necessary medical care; andWhereas, The United States Congress originally enacted the health care premium tax credits through the American Rescue Plan and extended the tax credits in the Inflation Reduction Act, making health insurance more accessible and affordable for more than 64,800 state residents; andWhereas, The enhanced premium tax credits are set to expire at the end of 2025 unless Congress acts; andWhereas, The expiration of the enhanced premium tax credits will result in the more than doubling of health insurance premiums for tens of thousands of state residents and will result in residents losing the ability to afford health insurance altogether; andWhereas, The expiration of the enhanced premium tax credits will force many state residents to choose between health care and other basic needs, such as housing and food, while also increasing reliance on state-funded assistance programs and emergency medical services, thereby straining the state's budget and resources; andWhereas, The continuation of enhanced premium tax credits is vital for maintaining economic stability, lowering health care costs for families, and ensuring that state residents do not delay or forgo critical medical care; andWhereas, Reduced enrollment in the state's individual health insurance marketplace could destabilize risk pools, leading to higher premiums and the potential for more carriers to leave the already limited health insurance provider market in the state; andWhereas, Failure to extend the enhanced premium tax credits will have severe economic and health consequences for families, individuals, the sustainability of the state's health insurance marketplace, and the economy of the state as a whole; therefore, be itResolved by the House of Delegates:That the West Virginia House of Delegates respectfully urges the United States Congress to act swiftly through budget reconciliation or other legislative means to extend beyond 2025 the enhanced premium tax credits for health insurance premiums under the Affordable Care Act; and, be itFurther Resolved, That, the West Virginia House of Delegates calls on the state's congressional delegation to champion the extension of enhanced premium tax credits, prioritizing the health and economic stability of the state's residents by ensuring that affordable health care remains accessible to all; and, be itFurther Resolved, That copies of this Resolution shall be sent to the Honorable Donald J. Trump, President of the United States; the Honorable Shelley Moore Capito and the Honorable Jim Justice, U.S. Senators; and the Honorable Carol Miller and the Honorable Alex Moore, U.S. Representatives, members of the West Virginia delegation in Congress.
