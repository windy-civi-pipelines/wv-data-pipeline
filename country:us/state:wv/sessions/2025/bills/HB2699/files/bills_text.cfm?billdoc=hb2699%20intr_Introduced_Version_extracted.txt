Red = new code to be enacted WEST VIRGINIA LEGISLATURE2025 REGULAR SESSION FISCAL NOTE IntroducedHouse Bill 2699By Delegates Horst, Ridenour, Hornby, Masters, Crouse, White, Petitto, Bridges, and Miller[Introduced February 20, 2025; referred to the Committee on Finance]A BILL to repeal §19-20-1, §19-20-2, and §19-20-11 of the Code of West Virginia, 1931, as amended, relating to repealing the sections of code relating to dogs being subject to taxation.Be it enacted by the Legislature of West Virginia: ARTICLE 20. DOGS AND CATS. §19-20-1. Dogs subject to taxation; declared to be personal property. [Repealed]. §19-20-2. Collection of head tax on dogs; duties of assessor and sheriff; registration of dogs; disposition of head tax; taxes on dogs not collected by assessor. [Repealed]. §19-20-11. Assessment of dogs as personal property. [Repealed]. NOTE: The purpose of this bill is to repeal the sections of code relating to dogs being subject to taxation.Strike-throughs indicate language that would be stricken from a heading or the present law and underscoring indicates new language that would be added.
